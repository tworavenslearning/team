# Phase 2: Architecture and Standards

## Objective

Design and validate content structure, confirm applicable standards, and establish style requirements. Ensure the architecture supports the prioritized backlog and enables consistent, maintainable content development.

## Active Agents

- **Modular Content Architect**: Designs content structure, modules, and reuse patterns. Ensures content is modular, reusable, and aligned with learning objectives.
- **Standards Enforcer**: Validates content against organizational standards (accessibility, branding, compliance). Identifies gaps and remediation requirements.
- **Style Guide Guardian**: Confirms and documents style requirements (tone, terminology, formatting). Ensures consistency across content assets.

## Inputs

- Prioritized Update Backlog from Phase 1
- Scope Agreement Document
- Correlation Report (audit vs. feedback)
- Existing content architecture (if any)
- Organizational standards documentation (accessibility, branding, compliance)
- Style guide and terminology glossary (if existing)

## Activities

1. **Architecture Blueprint Design**
   - Modular Content Architect maps backlog items to content structure.
   - Define modules, lessons, and reusable components.
   - Establish reuse patterns and content relationships (prerequisites, dependencies).
   - Document structure in architecture blueprint.

2. **Standards Validation**
   - Standards Enforcer reviews architecture against organizational standards.
   - Confirm accessibility (WCAG), branding, and compliance requirements.
   - Flag any architecture elements that conflict with standards.
   - Produce standards compliance checklist for Phase 3.

3. **Style and Terminology Confirmation**
   - Style Guide Guardian reviews and updates style requirements.
   - Confirm terminology, tone, and formatting rules.
   - Resolve conflicts between existing style and new requirements.
   - Publish or update style guide and terminology glossary.

4. **Blueprint Approval**
   - Present architecture blueprint to stakeholders.
   - Walk through structure, standards, and style requirements.
   - Obtain formal approval before development begins.
   - Document any approved exceptions or waivers.

5. **Development Readiness Package**
   - Compile architecture blueprint, standards checklist, and style guide.
   - Provide templates and examples for Phase 3 agents.
   - Define handoff format for content updates.

## Quality Gate

- Architecture blueprint approved by stakeholders.
- Standards requirements confirmed and documented.
- Style and terminology requirements confirmed and documented.
- Development readiness package complete.
- No unresolved conflicts between architecture and standards.

## Outputs

- Architecture Blueprint (structure, modules, reuse patterns)
- Standards Compliance Checklist
- Style Guide (tone, terminology, formatting)
- Terminology Glossary
- Development Templates and Examples
- Blueprint Approval Record

## Handoff Instructions

1. Deliver the Architecture Blueprint as the primary design document for Phase 3.
2. Provide the Standards Compliance Checklist as a mandatory review item during development.
3. Include the Style Guide and Terminology Glossary as reference for all content creation.
4. Supply templates and examples to accelerate development and ensure consistency.
5. Document any approved exceptions so Phase 3 agents do not re-open resolved decisions.
6. Specify the format and metadata required for content handoff to Phase 4.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Over-engineering**: Architecture too complex for scope. Mitigation: Match architecture complexity to backlog size; prefer simpler structures for smaller updates.
- **Standards conflicts**: Architecture cannot satisfy all standards. Mitigation: Escalate for waiver or compromise; document trade-offs.
- **Style guide gaps**: Unclear or contradictory style rules. Mitigation: Style Guide Guardian must resolve before handoff; create decision log for edge cases.
- **Stakeholder drift**: Approval delayed or conditional. Mitigation: Lock scope; treat new requests as change requests for next cycle.

## Duration Guidance

1-2 weeks. Architecture design typically requires 3-5 days; standards and style confirmation add 2-3 days. Stakeholder review and approval may extend to 2 weeks for complex or cross-functional content. Do not proceed to Phase 3 without blueprint approval.
