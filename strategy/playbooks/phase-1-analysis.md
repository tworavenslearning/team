# Phase 1: Analysis and Planning

## Objective

Analyze findings from Phase 0 and feedback data to produce a prioritized update backlog. Resolve contradictions and secure agreement on scope before architecture and development begin.

## Active Agents

- **Learner Intelligence Analyst**: Synthesizes learner feedback, completion patterns, and support ticket data. Identifies knowledge gaps, confusion points, and unmet learning needs.
- **Feedback Synthesizer**: Aggregates and correlates feedback from multiple sources (LMS, surveys, support tickets). Surfaces patterns and prioritizes learner-reported issues.

## Inputs

- Phase 0 audit reports (Content Drift Report, Quality Assessment Report, Adoption Baseline Report)
- Updated Content Inventory from Phase 0
- LMS feedback exports (ratings, comments, completion data)
- Support ticket data (tagged for learning or enablement issues)
- Stakeholder input on priorities and constraints

## Activities

1. **Correlate Audit Findings with Feedback**
   - Map Phase 0 drift and quality findings to learner feedback themes.
   - Identify content where audit and feedback align (high confidence) vs. diverge (requires investigation).

2. **Prioritize Update Backlog**
   - Learner Intelligence Analyst scores each update candidate by impact (adoption, support, compliance) and effort.
   - Apply prioritization matrix (e.g., impact vs. effort or RICE).
   - Produce ranked backlog with clear rationale for ordering.

3. **Resolve Contradictions**
   - Flag cases where audit findings conflict with learner feedback or stakeholder priorities.
   - Facilitate resolution with subject matter experts or stakeholders.
   - Document decisions and rationale for scope inclusions and exclusions.

4. **Scope Agreement**
   - Present prioritized backlog to stakeholders for approval.
   - Confirm scope boundaries, resource allocation, and timeline expectations.
   - Obtain sign-off on backlog before proceeding to Phase 2.

5. **Backlog Refinement**
   - Finalize backlog items with acceptance criteria.
   - Define dependencies (e.g., Module A must precede Module B).
   - Assign preliminary effort estimates.

## Quality Gate

- Prioritized backlog generated and documented.
- Contradictions resolved with documented rationale.
- Scope agreed and signed off by stakeholders.
- Backlog items have acceptance criteria and effort estimates.
- Dependencies identified and sequenced.

## Outputs

- Prioritized Update Backlog (with impact/effort scores)
- Contradiction Resolution Log
- Scope Agreement Document (signed)
- Correlation Report (audit findings vs. feedback)
- Refined Backlog with acceptance criteria and dependencies

## Handoff Instructions

1. Deliver the approved Prioritized Update Backlog as the primary input for Phase 2.
2. Include the Scope Agreement Document to bound architecture and development decisions.
3. Provide the Correlation Report for context on why certain items were prioritized.
4. Highlight any constraints (e.g., compliance deadlines, audience-specific requirements) that affect architecture.
5. Ensure backlog items are sufficiently detailed for Phase 2 agents to design and validate structure.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Feedback data gaps**: LMS or support ticket data lacks sufficient granularity. Mitigation: Use qualitative data (surveys, interviews) to supplement; document assumptions.
- **Stakeholder misalignment**: Scope disagreement persists. Mitigation: Escalate to decision authority; use phased scope with clear "Phase 1" vs. "Phase 2" deliverables.
- **Over-prioritization**: Backlog too large for available capacity. Mitigation: Apply strict cutline; defer lower-priority items to next cycle.
- **Vague acceptance criteria**: Backlog items too ambiguous for Phase 2. Mitigation: Run backlog refinement workshop before handoff; require "definition of done" per item.

## Duration Guidance

1 week. Analysis and prioritization typically require 3-4 days; stakeholder alignment and scope agreement may add 2-3 days. Avoid extending into Phase 2; scope creep is more costly after architecture begins.

## Notes

- The prioritized backlog is the primary constraint for all subsequent phases; invest time in getting it right.
- Maintain traceability from backlog items back to audit findings and feedback sources for future reference.
